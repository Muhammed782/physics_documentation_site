Physics

Introduction

Physics is the branch of science that studies matter, energy, and the interaction between them. It helps us understand how the universe behaves, from the motion of everyday objects to the behavior of light and atoms.

Kinematics

Kinematics describes motion without considering the forces that cause it. It involves quantities such as displacement, velocity, and acceleration.

Newton's Laws

1. An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a force.

2. Force equals mass times acceleration (F = ma).

3. For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Work & Energy

Work is done when a force causes movement. Energy is the ability to do work and exists in different forms such as kinetic and potential energy.

Waves

Waves transfer energy from one place to another without transferring matter. Examples include sound waves and light waves.
